# Research

## Research Overview

My research lies at the intersection of observational astronomy, astrometry, and data analysis, with a strong emphasis on Solar System applications. A central goal of my work is to use precise measurements to improve the physical and dynamical characterization of small bodies and related phenomena.

## Stellar Occultations

Stellar occultations provide one of the most precise ground-based techniques for studying small bodies in the Solar System. Through these events, it is possible to constrain sizes, shapes, atmospheres, rings, and satellites with high accuracy. My work in this area involves event prediction, observational coordination, data reduction, and scientific interpretation.

## Astrometry

Astrometry plays a central role in my research. I work on precise positional measurements and their application to orbit improvement, ephemeris refinement, and the interpretation of close encounters and occultation geometries. I am also interested in methodological aspects related to reference systems and apparent positions.

## Solar System Small Bodies

A major part of my research focuses on small bodies, including trans-Neptunian objects, centaurs, irregular satellites, and asteroids. I am interested in their physical properties, orbital behavior, and observational characterization through astrometry and occultations.

## Microlensing and Prediction Methods

I am also involved in the prediction and analysis of rare astronomical events, including close stellar encounters and microlensing candidates. This work includes probability-based selection methods, uncertainty propagation, and observational feasibility analysis.

## Scientific Software

An important component of my work is the development of scientific software to support astronomical research. This includes tools for occultation reduction and analysis, reproducible computational workflows, and resources that help connect observations, modeling, and interpretation.

## Collaboration

I am interested in collaborations involving observational campaigns, event prediction, astrometry, occultation analysis, and scientific software development.
